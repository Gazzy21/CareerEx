Job Requirements

Being an Astronaut means you must have:

  • Physical Fitness
  • Technical Expertise
  • Teamwork and Communication
  • Problem-Solving Skills
  • Adaptability
  • Risk Management
  • Emotional Resilience

Being an Astronaut means you can get paid handsomely and recieve good benefits:

Starting Salary: New astronauts, depending on their education and experience, typically start at the GS-12 pay grade. As of 2023, a GS-12 salary ranges from approximately $71,099 to $92,429 per year.

Experienced Astronauts: More experienced astronauts can advance to the GS-13 and GS-14 pay grades. A GS-13 salary ranges from approximately $84,546 to $109,908 per year, and a GS-14 salary ranges from approximately $99,908 to $129,878 per year.

Senior Astronauts: Those with extensive experience and seniority might reach the GS-15 pay grade, which ranges from approximately $117,191 to $152,352 per year.

Additional Benefits: Besides base salary, astronauts receive various benefits, including health insurance, retirement plans, and allowances for housing and travel.
